Why MC1 (DTM) and MC2 (Boundaries)

Population/DTM appeared as an FRQ topic in 5 of the last 6 exams. Boundaries appeared in 4 of 6. 2024 tested both. Units 2+4 = 24-34% of the exam. At least 1 of 3 FRQs every year comes from these units — 7 points, a third of the FRQ section. 62% of our students are below 80% on Unit 4. 50% below 80% on Unit 2. We targeted the highest-frequency, highest-weight content first.

What the S3 Data Showed

FRQ determines the score. Every AP 5 had FRQ ≥77%. Every 1-3 had FRQ ≤63%. MCQ doesn't discriminate at the top.

Students collapse when topics mix. Branson: 88% topic quizzes → 24% FRQ (64-point drop). He completed 100% of the course. It's not effort — the course produced recognition without retrieval.

The base course had structural issues. 27.3% of questions were 4-choice (exam is 5-choice). Stimulus coverage 24.4% (exam is 30-40%). Skill 4 at 6.2% (target 13-20%). The mini-courses fix all three.

Research & Insights We Produced

Deep research was done across CollegeBoard releases, Reddit, teacher blogs, prep books, and AP community sources. All findings are in the repo. Here's what we found and what documents exist:

Exam Architecture Research (ap_hugeo_ground_reality.md)
Independent research on exam structure: 60 MCQ + 3 FRQ, 7 units, 68 topics, 5 CED skills with weight ranges, 8 MCQ question types, FRQ verb set (Define, Describe, Identify, Compare, Explain), stimulus types and coverage, score conversion tables.

Released FRQ Analysis 2019-2024 (gap_analysis_s4_vs_real_exam.md)
Every released FRQ mapped by topic and unit. Found DTM in 5/6 years, Boundaries in 4/6. FRQs routinely cross multiple units in a single question. Stimulus types: thematic maps ~30%, data tables ~15%, pyramids ~10%.

Prep Book Analysis — 906 Questions Extracted (prep_books_deep_dive.md)
Barron's 2022/2024 (~585 unique MCQs, 96.5% overlap between editions) + Princeton Review 15th Ed (267 MCQs). Princeton has 35% more content words, 2.4x more key terms. Both have answer position bias and low stimulus coverage. Grand total: ~1,489 MCQs, ~104 FRQs across all 3 books.

Reddit, Teacher Blogs, AP Community (documented in brain dump)
DTM universally reported as most tested model. Centripetal/centrifugal vs push/pull is a common confusion point (matches Branson's error). FRQ practice is the #1 recommended study method. Heimler's History is the #1 video resource. AP Classroom Progress Checks are the closest exam proxy. 2024 Chief Reader: spatial analysis is #1 missed skill nationally.

Question Bank Audit (mcq_deep_analysis.md)
546 questions audited across MC1/MC2/MC3/B1. Found 27.3% are 4-choice, answer A bias at 42.5%, stimulus coverage 24.4%, Skill 4 critically underrepresented at 6.2%.

S3 Student Analysis (s3_review/ap_human_geo_s3_review.md)
10 students analyzed with per-student CED skill breakdowns, FRQ grading, qualitative error coding, recognition-retrieval gap measurement, and 3-layer intervention plans.

Comprehensive Course Review (mc1_comprehensive_review.md)
Full pedagogical review of MC1 course design: learning flow, question quality, FRQ structure, stimulus coverage, skill distribution.
