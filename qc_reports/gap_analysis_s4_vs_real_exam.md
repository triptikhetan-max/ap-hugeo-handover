## PART 1: GROUND REALITY — What the Real AP Exam Looks Like

*Sources: College Board CED, AP Central, Barron's, Fiveable, UWorld, PrepScholar, Albert.io*

---

### 1.1 Exam Structure

| Section | Questions | Time | Weight |
|---|---|---|---|
| Section I: MCQ | 60 questions | 60 minutes (1 min/Q) | 50% |
| Section II: FRQ | 3 questions (7 pts each) | 75 minutes (~25 min/Q) | 50% |
| **Total** | | **135 minutes** | **100%** |

- **Fully digital** — taken in Bluebook testing app
- **No penalty** for wrong answers — answer every question
- **No calculator** allowed on either section
- Composite score: MCQ → 60 pts + FRQ → 60 pts (scaled) = 120 composite → scaled to 1-5

---

### 1.2 MCQ Format — CRITICAL FACTS

| Dimension | Real AP Exam |
|---|---|
| **Choices per question** | **Always 5 (A through E)** |
| **Stimulus-based questions** | **30-40%** (~18-24 out of 60) |
| **Stimulus format** | 5-8 sets of 2-3 questions each + standalone questions |
| **Stimulus types** | Maps, tables, charts, graphs, images, infographics, landscapes |
| **Quantitative vs Qualitative** | Roughly evenly divided |
| **Answer position balance** | Well-balanced (~20% each for A-E) |

**Key insight from College Board:** "Approximately 30-40% of the multiple-choice questions will reference stimulus material, including maps, tables, charts, graphs, images, infographics, and/or landscapes, roughly evenly divided between quantitative and qualitative sources."

---

### 1.3 Eight MCQ Question Types (per Barron's)

| # | Type | Description | Speed |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Stimulus-Response** | Interpret a map/graph/image, then answer | Slow (60-90s) |
| 2 | **Definitional** | Identify/define a concept, model, or theory | Fast (20-30s) |
| 3 | **Cause-and-Effect** | Given a cause, identify the correct effect | Medium |
| 4 | **Generalization** | From a specific case, identify the general principle | Medium |
| 5 | **Solution to Problem** | Define + apply a concept to solve a problem | Medium-Slow |
| 6 | **Hypothetical Situation** | Apply knowledge to a hypothetical scenario | Medium |
| 7 | **Chronological** | Order/date notable events or processes | Fast |
| 8 | **Compare/Contrast** | Compare two events, models, or concepts | Medium |

**From 2020 onward, stimulus-based questions comprise roughly HALF of all MCQs.**

### 1.6 FRQ Structure

| FRQ | Stimulus | Points | Structure |
|---|---|---|---|
| Q1 | None (text scenario only) | 7 (parts A-G) | Define → Describe → Explain |
| Q2 | One stimulus (map/chart/image) | 7 (parts A-G) | Interpret → Apply → Analyze |
| Q3 | Two stimuli (map + data, etc.) | 7 (parts A-G) | Compare → Synthesize → Evaluate |

- Task verbs: Define, Describe, Identify, Compare, Explain
- At least 2 of 3 FRQs test spatial analysis skills

## PART 3: GAP ANALYSIS — Where We Diverge from Reality

---

### GAP 1: 🔴 CRITICAL — 27% of Questions are 4-Choice (AP uses ONLY 5-choice)

| | Real AP | Our Builds |
|---|---|---|
| Choice count | 100% five-choice (A-E) | 71.6% five-choice, **27.3% four-choice** |

**Impact:** Students practicing with 4-choice questions are training on wrong format. On the real exam, they must evaluate 5 options. This affects:
- Time management (5 choices takes longer to evaluate)
- Elimination strategy (eliminating 4 wrong answers vs 3)
- Guessing probability (20% vs 25%)

**Root cause:** 149 legacy HUMG20 items imported from Princeton/Barron's prep books.

**Fix:** Remove all 149 four-choice items and replace with AP Classroom 5-choice items, OR add a plausible 5th distractor to each and rebalance answer keys.

---

### GAP 2: 🔴 CRITICAL — Answer-A Bias (42.5% vs ~20% expected)

| | Real AP | Our Builds |
|---|---|---|
| Answer A correct | ~20% (balanced) | **42.5%** |
| Answer B-E correct | ~20% each | 13-15% each |

**Impact:** This is a testing integrity disaster. A student who always picks A would get 42.5% correct — nearly passing without reading a single question. Test-savvy students will quickly notice the pattern.

**Root cause:** Legacy HUMG20 4-choice items have ~90% answer-A bias (the correct answer is almost always listed first in the import).

**Fix:** After removing/fixing 4-choice items, audit remaining 5-choice items. Randomize correct answer positions so each position (A-E) is correct 18-22% of the time.

---

### GAP 3: 🔴 SIGNIFICANT — Stimulus Coverage Too Low (24.4% vs 30-40% target)

| | Real AP | Our Builds |
|---|---|---|
| Stimulus coverage | 30-40% | **24.4%** overall |
| MC3 specifically | — | **5.3%** (critically low) |
| Stimulus sets (2-3 Qs per stimulus) | 5-8 sets per exam | Mostly 1:1 |

**Impact:** Students aren't being trained on the hardest part of the AP exam. Stimulus interpretation questions are the ones that take longest and differentiate scores.

**Fix:**
- MC3 needs urgent stimulus addition (currently 6 out of 114 questions!)
- Add map-based, data-table, and passage stimuli
- Build **stimulus sets** (2-3 questions per stimulus) to match AP format
- Target stimulus types: Maps (~30%), Tables (~15%), Passages (~15%), Images (~10%), Diagrams (~10%)

---

### GAP 4: 🟡 MODERATE — Skill Imbalance (S1 overloaded, S4/S5 underserved)

| Skill | CED Target | Our Builds | Gap |
|---|---|---|---|
| S1: Concepts | 25-36% | **42.5%** | +6.5% over max |
| S4: Source Analysis | 13-20% | **6.2%** | -6.8% below min |
| S5: Scale Analysis | 13-20% | **8.4%** | -4.6% below min |

**Impact:** Too many "define this concept" questions, not enough "analyze this source" or "how does this look at different scales" questions. S4 and S5 are the higher-order skills that differentiate 4s and 5s.

**Fix:** Adding more stimulus-based questions naturally increases S3/S4/S5. Specifically:
- S4 (Source Analysis): Add questions requiring students to evaluate the reliability/utility of maps, data, images
- S5 (Scale Analysis): Add questions asking how patterns change at local vs national vs global scales

---

### GAP 5: 🟡 MODERATE — Source Quality (56% AP Classroom vs 70%+ target)

| | Target | Our Builds |
|---|---|---|
| AP Classroom | 70%+ | 56% |
| MC3 specifically | 70%+ | **0%** |

**Impact:** Non-AP sources (Princeton, Barron's, unknown QTI) don't perfectly match AP style, vocabulary, or rigor. They train students on subtly different expectations.

**Fix:** Replace legacy items with AP Classroom questions, especially in MC3 which has zero AP Classroom content.

---

### GAP 6: 🟡 MINOR — B1 Difficulty All "Medium"

B1 (Mini Practice Tests) has all 120 questions tagged as "medium" difficulty. A real AP exam has a mix.

**Fix:** Likely a metadata issue. Review and retag with appropriate difficulty levels.

---

### GAP 7: 🟡 MINOR — No FRQs in Any S4 Build

Real AP exam is 50% FRQ (3 questions, 21 total points). None of our S4 builds include FRQ practice.

**Context:** This may be by design (FRQs handled separately), but worth flagging.

---

### GAP 8: 📋 INFO — 522/546 Questions Missing Answer Feedback

96% of questions have no answer explanations/feedback. For a learning product (not just testing), this limits educational value.
